Swipe Me Hunger Mobile Website


Have you ever visited a website on your smartphone and didn’t have an enjoyable experience? Many times the images that look great on the desktop version might become too overwhelming on your tiny screen. Or maybe the navigation is completely everywhere and the texts are just taking up way too much space than is necessary.

For the Swipe Me Hunger’s website, this was the case as their mobile version of their website wasn’t too user friendly. As a user experience designer, I created a much pleasing visual design using figma to properly address the issues. Swipe Me Hunger’s website didn’t translate well when using a smartphone as many of the blocks of white space and images were way too big in proportion to the screen. Users would have to scroll much longer to accommodate the inconvenient mobile design. Not only that, text sizing looks much larger on their mobile version.

The overall sizing of the mobile website proved to steer readers away, thus, defeating the purpose of the whole website that is helping others not have food scarcity. To combat this issue, I removed unnecessary color blocks, images, and re-sized the entire page to have a minimized version of the website. Not only is the most significant information now the first thing the user sees, the navigation and content are now visible.
