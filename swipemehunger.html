Swipe Me Hunger Mobile Website


Have you ever visited a website on your smartphone and didn’t have an enjoyable experience? Many times the images that look great on the desktop version might become too overwhelming on your tiny mobile screen. The navigation menu might look displaced, or the texts may take up way too much space than is necessary. For the Swipe Me Hunger’s website, this was the case as their mobile version of their website wasn’t user friendly.

Swipe Me Hunger is a website that is dedicated to helping students with food insecurity. Food insecurity is a significant issue for students who are struggling to balance their health while attending college. Swipe Me Hunger provides resources to students who have low income, or don't have the transportation to purchase food.

As a user experience designer, I created a mockup of the mobile design of the Swipe Me Hunger’s website. There were blocks of white space and the images were too big in proportion to the mobile screen. Users were forced to scroll much longer to accommodate the inconvenient mobile design. Also, the text sizing looked much larger on their mobile version in comparison to their desktop website.

To find a solution to these problems, I removed the unnecessary color blocks, images, and had re-sized the entire page to have a proper version of the website. My mockup provides the most significant information first and the content is visibly more clear.
