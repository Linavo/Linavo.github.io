Ulta's Content Strategy


Click here to view Ulta's Content Strategy

Every respective company has a great content strategist. Many may take on several roles within the company or be specialized in content strategy itself. For my proposed content strategy for Ulta, I explored the many factors that contribute to a great plan. The proposal itself provides the interactor profile, which identifies the general demographic of people who are consumers at the company, and content fitness.

I’ve also gone so far as to dig a little deeper and understand more about their competitors, Sephora and Sally’s Beauty Supply. For this project, I went on each competitor’s website and compared their features, targeted audiences, and content with Ulta’s. After gaining insight of the various similarities and differences they all have, I was able to make the conclusion that Ulta’s greatest strengths relied on maintaining current trends comparatively to the other two major corporations. Both Sephora and Sally’s Beauty Supply had maintained a similar image in the past years; although it hasn’t significantly decreased their consumers, Ulta has significantly gained more customers in these past years. In my personal experiences, I loved to shop at all three of the stores for different products, but Ulta had always been the store I knew I could rely on for anything. Throughout my proposed content strategy, I specified how Ulta’s wide variety of products may cater to a bigger audience than their audiences.

Not only that, it gives them the flexibility to advertise towards different minority groups. Great companies are always revisiting their marketing strategies and user experience approaches to cater to the public.
